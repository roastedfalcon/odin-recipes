French Fries

Description

These are the best homemade french fries from russet potatoes. These are easy to make and they go perfectly with chicken tenders!

Ingredients

  • 10 large russet potatoes, peeled and rinsed
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil, can sub canola oil
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Slice potatoes into 1/2 inch thick sticks.
  2. Soak potato slices in cold water for at least one hour, at most overnight. Longer is better as this process removes the starch making our fries crispier.
  3. Rinse fries twice with cold water.
  4. Lay fries on paper towels and pat them as dry as possible.
  5. Heat oil in a deep fryer or dutch oven to 300 degrees F. Frying will be done in about six batches. Begin with a quart of oil and add more as needed.
  6. Use a slotted spoon to add the potatoes to the oil and fry for about 5-6 minutes. The potatoes should be heating and not crisping yet.
  7. Place the cooked potatoes on a power towel and fry the remaining bathces.
  8. Increase the heat to 400 degrees F and fry each batch a second time until they are crisp and golden brown, about 5 minutes.
  9. Remove and place on dry paper towels. Sprinkle with salt.
